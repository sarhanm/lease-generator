### 1.16 Fire and Safety

The Resident agrees to prevent the risk of fire and fire-related injuries. This includes following these minimum guidelines:

a) No combustible fluids, fireworks, or other materials that could cause a fire, explosion, or health hazard shall be kept or used in or around the property. No fireworks or explosives shall be ignited on the Property.

b) Maintain smoke detectors and CO detectors by replacing batteries every six (6) months (unless fitted with long-life lithium batteries), vacuuming the dust from openings regularly, and testing monthly.

c) Report smoke detector problems to the Owner immediately.

d) Use electrical appliances and tools which have a UL or other recognized testing laboratory's label.

e) Unplug small appliances and portable heaters when not in use.

f) Discontinue use of any item with a cracked or frayed electrical cord.

g) Minimize the use of extension cords and do not overload outlets; use multi-plug adaptors that have surge protection.

h) Use light bulbs that are the appropriate size and type for lamps and fixtures.

i) Keep electrical cords out from beneath furniture and rugs.

j) Keep all items a recommended twelve inches (12") or more away from baseboard heaters.

k) Place portable heaters at least three (3) feet from bedding, furniture, and other flammable materials.

l) Never leave cooking or other burning items unattended.

m) If grease catches fire, carefully slide a lid over the pan to smother flames.

n) Develop household emergency escape plans, including where applicable, the purchase of roll-up escape ladders.

o) Ensure all household members are able to dial 911 for police, fire, and medical assistance, including knowing the address of the Property.

### 6.2 What Are Molds?

Molds are microscopic organisms that produce enzymes to digest organic matter and spores to reproduce. They are part of the fungi kingdom. Problems arise when mold starts digesting organic materials in our homes.

### 6.3 How Do Molds Grow in My Home?

Once mold spores settle in your home, they need moisture to begin growing. There are molds that can grow on wood, ceiling tiles, wallpaper, paints, carpet, sheetrock, and insulation. The way to control mold growth is to control moisture.

### 6.4 How Can Molds Affect My Health?

You may experience a skin rash, running nose, eye irritation, cough, congestion, and aggravation of asthma. If you have immune suppression or underlying lung disease, you may be at increased risk for infections from molds. If you or your family members have health problems that you suspect are caused by exposure to mold, you should consult with your physician.

### 6.5 How to Control Mold Growth

- Fix any moisture problems: stop all water leaks, repair leaking roofs and plumbing.
- Increase air circulation within your home, especially along the inside of exterior walls.
- Install and use exhaust fans in bathrooms, kitchens, and laundry rooms.
- Ventilate and insulate the attic and crawlspaces.
- Clean and dry water-damaged carpets, clothing, bedding, and upholstered furniture within 24 to 48 hours.
- Vacuum and clean your home regularly.

### 6.6 How to Clean Up Mold

For **large areas**, consider hiring a professional. If cleaning yourself: protect yourself with goggles, gloves, and breathing protection. Seal off the area from the rest of your home. Scrub all affected hard surfaces first with a mild detergent solution, then optionally with a bleach solution (¼ cup bleach to one quart of water), and finally apply a borate-based detergent solution and do not rinse.

For **small areas**, follow the same process with a cotton dust mask. Bag all moldy materials being discarded.

### 11.3 Winterizing Water Pipes

When freezing weather is expected, take the following precautions:

1. Keep room temperature at a level to sufficiently warm the walls containing water pipes.
2. Leave cabinet doors open below kitchen and bathroom sinks to allow room heat to circulate.
3. Cold water taps may be left running at a light trickle when temperatures are expected to plunge.
4. Pipes in the exterior walls leading to outdoor faucets are extremely susceptible to freezing. If a shut-off valve services particular outdoor faucets, they should be completely disconnected. Wrap outdoor faucets or provide commercial insulation.
